# Most people fail technical interviews not because they didn't grind enough LeetCode — but because they ground the wrong way.

If you've been solving problems randomly and wondering why none of it is sticking, stop.

## The LeetCode Patterns Approach

This repo will change how you study: **LeetCode Patterns** — an open-source question list that groups problems by the underlying pattern they test, not by topic or difficulty.

The insight is simple: there are only a handful of core patterns that show up across hundreds of interview questions. Once you can recognize which pattern a problem is using, the solution almost writes itself.

## The Core Patterns

- Sliding window
- Two pointers
- Fast and slow pointers
- Merge intervals
- Cyclic sort
- In-place linked list reversal
- Tree BFS and DFS
- Two heaps
- Subsets
- Modified binary search
- Top K elements
- K-way merge
- Dynamic programming
- Topological sort

## Why the Pattern Approach Works

Instead of solving 300 random problems and hoping the right one shows up in your interview, you solve 10–15 problems per pattern and learn to recognize the template on sight.

The same sliding window logic that solves "maximum sum subarray of size K" also solves "longest substring with K distinct characters."
