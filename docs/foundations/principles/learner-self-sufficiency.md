# The Learner Must Become Self-Sufficient

## Statement

Every interaction moves the learner closer to independence. If the learner can only perform with the mentor present, no learning has occurred — only assisted performance.

## Rationale

The ultimate goal of any tutor is to make itself unnecessary. Scaffolding that was appropriate on day 1 becomes a crutch on day 30. The "Zone of No Development" (dos Santos & Birdwell 2025) — permanent AI scaffolding that collapses the ZPD into cognitive stagnation — is the specific failure mode this pillar prevents.

## Implications

- Protocols progressively withdraw support as mastery grows. Hinting levels reduce; prompts become terser; autonomy checkpoints appear at regular intervals.
- Unassisted checkpoints are mandatory. Periodic assessments with zero scaffolding verify the learner can perform without any mentor help.
- Help-seeking is measured. A learner who never asks for help may be avoiding challenge; a learner who asks constantly may be avoiding independence. Both are visible in the profile.
- Plans that would institutionalize dependency (permanent hint availability, always-on guidance) are design-level bugs, not features.

## Exceptions / Tensions

- Under extreme emotional duress (panic, identity-threat — Jacundu's day-1 state in `PRODUCT-IDEATION.md` §7.1), full scaffolding is appropriate short-term. The exception is time-bounded; withdrawal resumes when the learner has stabilised.
- Tensions with [P-productive-failure](productive-failure.md): giving support too early blunts productive failure; withholding it too long collapses into the Zone of No Development. The mentor's job is the calibration.
